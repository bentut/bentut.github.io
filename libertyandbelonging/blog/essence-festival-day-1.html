ESSENCE Festival (Day 2)

7/7/2019

0 Comments

 
Click image to see all photos
I was most excited to visit the “Power Conference” stage at Essence Fest where black artists and leaders share inspiration, ideas, and stories about changing the world. On the early afternoon agenda were panels with titles like: “The Black Effect: How our culture dictates what is next from creation to consumption” and “For colored girls who have considered politics”. I was especially interested in “Breaking the cycle of silence to heal our communities” with Terry Crews and Common. I missed them all.
Between the parking insanity (anticipated but nevertheless completely helpless against) and the enormity of the conference (unanticipated and completely unprepared for) I was about an hour and a half later than the 4 hour late start I was already dealing with. I tried to take the sage advice of my new friend Sabrina and tell myself that I was exactly where I was supposed to be, but the hot sun and getting frustratingly lost in New Orleans’s theme-park sized convention center made it difficult.

What made it easier was the fact that Essence Fest was straight up incredible. As a frequenter of conference type gatherings and the occasional festival, the rest of the gathering world can learn a lot from how black folks get together for Essence fest. In no particular order, some of the things that stood out to me #1 families attending. Legit multigenerational, all ages, keiki to kupuna. #2 a dedication to style and beauty that was also so various #3 the spectacle #4 the awesome T-shirt’s (my favorite was on a man and read: Dear Police, I am a white woman”). I love the way the world looks and feels at Essence. And it has not ever felt like that to me before.

in so many ways I cannot believe my own dumb luck. I planned my trip around a moon cycle and an unrelated political statement. In any year, going to this festival would have been a once in a lifetime kind of opportunity. In this particular year on this learning excursion about the legacy of America’s treatment of race, it is surreal.

Essence fest is huge. Unfathomably so. I recommend learning about it and tracking the conversations and performance there. For me, it helped me stay sane to focus on the Power Conference. When I eventually did get there, i caught some amazing, rich and challenging conversations.

One called #blackdadsmatter was an emotional experience for me - I was in tears as I remembered something that I forgot almost 15 years ago. How much I want to be a father. Those men are who I want to be as a dad. I also got to see Ava Duvernay, a voice in film that I am in awe of, talk about her work telling stories of the incareral state. Each of these panels warrants a blog post of its own. Suffice it to say, by the end of the day it was no longer hard to convince myself that I was exactly where I was supposed to be.

About

Removed from the skills, relationships, and resources I rely on to feel a part of community in Hawaii, I am traveling to Louisiana to volunteer with organizations working in black and native communities to find out what it means to belong to those peoples and those places that I have had no personal history with, yet whose histories have been so instrumental to the foundation of the only national identity I’ve ever known.

​The experience of being black in America is something I have no direct experience with and I question the validity of my participation in any discussion about it. At the same time, it’s a discussion that triggers me, that I am drawn to, and that i feel, for many reasons, is vital to the future of our nation, and how we govern, two things that I am definitely involved in.

I’m going to Louisiana to understand how I can be a part of fighting to fix everything that is wrong with how we treat people as Americans, how we treat our black neighbors, how we treat our indigenous forerunners — ultimately how we treat each other. In Louisiana — a place I’ve only visited once as an adult, and seemingly a world away from Hawaii — the kind of work we can be doing on these fronts is on full display

This individual learning excursion is part of my participation in The Omidyar Fellows Program. To learn more about other fellows’ ILEs visit this page.
